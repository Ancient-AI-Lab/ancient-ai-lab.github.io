### About Ancient AI Lab

I started this project because of a question that wouldn't leave me alone: did ancient civilizations have construction technologies we don't understand?

Not ancient aliens. Not crystal energy healing. A real engineering question -- how did people cut and move 100-ton granite blocks 4,500 years ago? The mainstream answer is "ramps and copper chisels." The alternative answer involves acoustic levitation, pyramid power, Tesla technology, and antigravity. Both camps have strong opinions and almost zero math.

So I built a lab to do the math.

### What I've actually tested

I've run finite element simulations on the specific claims that the ancient mysteries community cares about. Here's what I've found:

**Acoustic levitation** -- The core claim is that sound waves can lift stone blocks. I modeled acoustic radiation pressure on granite at 500 watts of water-coupled sound. The result: 0.1 newtons of force. That's real. That's measurable. And that's 245,000 times too weak to lift a pyramid block. (Phase 2, 4, 11)

**The Navy's antigravity patent** -- Patent 10,144,532, "Craft Using an Inertial Mass Reduction Device." The Navy spent over $500K testing it between 2016 and 2019. I built a computational model from the patent's own equations. The result: a 22-24 order-of-magnitude gap between what electromagnetic fields can produce and what you'd need to curve spacetime. The patent expired in 2023 when they stopped paying maintenance fees. (Phase 14)

**Pyramid power** -- Does the Great Pyramid's geometry concentrate energy? I ran a Helmholtz acoustic simulation. At one specific frequency (40 Hz), the pyramid amplifies pressure 10.77x. But averaged across a realistic frequency range? 0.904x. The pyramid makes things WORSE. Geometry alone doesn't concentrate fields -- you need wavelength-matched resonance. (Phase 13)

**Consciousness and quartz** -- Can the mind interact with quartz crystals? I modeled thermal decoherence in quartz at room temperature. The result: quantum coherence lasts 25 femtoseconds. That's 25 millionths of a billionth of a second. More than 10 orders of magnitude shorter than any consciousness timescale. (Phase 15)

**Tesla and electromagnetic gravity** -- Can electromagnetic fields bend gravity, as Tesla enthusiasts claim? The coupling constant between EM energy and spacetime curvature is G/c^4, roughly 10^-43. I calculated the maximum achievable metric perturbation: 10^-32. That's 23 orders of magnitude below Earth's gravitational field. The math is brutal. (Phase 12, 14)

**Coral Castle** -- Edward Leedskalnin's proposed magnetic and acoustic mechanisms don't bridge the force gap. But vibration-based friction reduction? That actually works. 50%+ friction reduction, experimentally verified. (Phase 11)

### What does work

Not everything fails. Acoustic radiation pressure is real and measurable. Vibration friction reduction is experimentally verified. Pyramid geometry does amplify at specific resonant frequencies. And I've screened 50 mechanism claims from 71+ sources -- 6 survived first-principles physics screening.

### How this works

The entire pipeline is built with Claude Code on a MacBook Air M4. I use SfePy and Gmsh for finite element simulations, PySR for symbolic regression, and NumPy/SciPy/SymPy for the analytical math. 15 completed research phases. 173 passing tests. 4,501 lines of Python. Every assumption stated. Every source cited. Every notebook on GitHub.

### Why I'm doing this

Because somebody should. There are real questions about ancient construction that deserve real physics -- not YouTube speculation and not academic dismissal. I don't care if an idea comes from a peer-reviewed journal or a Reddit comment. If it's a testable claim, I run the simulation. When the math says no, I say no.

### What if the gap isn't in their claims?

Every result I've published uses the physics we have today. Newton, Maxwell, Einstein, quantum mechanics. And almost every ancient mechanism I've tested comes up short -- sometimes by 22 orders of magnitude.

But here's what keeps me going: what if the gap isn't in their claims? What if it's in our math?

I'm not saying our physics is wrong. I'm saying I've now documented exactly where it would need to be different. Every OOM gap is a measurement of what we'd need to discover. Every "ruled out" is ruled out *within current frameworks*. If there's a new physics -- a new coupling constant, a nonlinear effect we haven't modeled, a mechanism we haven't named -- this is the data that would point to it.

That's why the code is open. That's why every assumption is stated. So when someone finds the thing I missed, they can prove it.

That's it. That's the lab.
